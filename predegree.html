Predegree/Basic Studies

ADMISSION THROUGH THE PRE-DEGREE PROGRAMME
Admission into the Pre-degree Programme is open to candidates with at least five credit passes, which must include English Language, Mathematics; Physics, Chemistry and Biology at not more than two sittings in the SSCE (WAECjNECO or GCE) or equivalent qualifications (Agricultural Science is acceptable in place of Biology of Agricultural Education and Agriculture candidates only). The candidates must have taken the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) for that year and scored a minimum aggregate of not less than 180. Admission into this programme is conducted once a year. Students who have successfully completed the programme are usually admitted into Degree Programmes in the faculties where they undertook the Pre-degree Programme. Admission of students into pre-degree programme is restricted to academic programmes in the Faculties of Science, Agriculture, Education (Science) which do not have sufficient candidates for admission through the UTME.

Subjects to be offered by Pre-degree students
Students offered admission into the Pre-degree programme are to offer the following subjects: English Language, Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics and Agricultural Science.

Criteria for post pre-degree placement
Post pre-degree students with acceptable level of performance are placed in the degree programmes for which the candidates were offered pre-degree admission in the first instance.

The Basic Studies programme is designed to prepare students for the examinations conducted by the consortium of the Joint Universities Preliminary Examination Board (JUPEB). Holders of the JUPEB certificate would subsequently be admitted into the 200 level of the degree offered by the Promaridrian University through Direct Entry JAMB.
